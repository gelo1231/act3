Intramurals

The world of college intramural sports awaits.

For many students, being out of high school and on a college campus marks the first time they're no longer involved in club sports or organized competitions. Many are also looking for college activities to meet new people.

What are Intramurals?

University-specific sports leagues, events, and tournaments that take place only on campus are known as intramurals. Intramurals provide a different experience from Rockhurst's NCAA Division II teams, which play intercollegiate sports versus other colleges, frequently with athletes on athletic scholarships. These student-run competitions are designed to offer fun, relaxation, and physical activity in a relaxed yet structured environment. Essentially, intramural sports create a venue for students to play sports for fun, fitness, and friendship inside the university, whereas intercollegiate sports serve as an official and frequently scholarship-driven athletic platform.

Types of Intramural Sports

Rockhurst offers co-ed leagues, activities, and tournaments in a wide range of team sports and competitions. They include:

  1. Soccer
  2. Basketball (5v5 indoor, 3v3 outdoor)
  3. Volleyball
  4. Sand volleyball
  5. Flag football
  6. Dodgeball
  7. Ping pong
  8. Tennis (singles and doubles)
  9. Cornhole
  10. Kickball

How Do Intramural Sports Work?

Students can freely register individually or as a team for various sports activities, with no associated costs. Registration can be completed on the IM Leagues Home Page. Game schedules are available online, and matches take place at different locations such as Burke Field, the area behind Corcoran Hall, inside the Convocation Center, or on the tennis courts. Teams are also encouraged to hold their own practices without any official commitments.
